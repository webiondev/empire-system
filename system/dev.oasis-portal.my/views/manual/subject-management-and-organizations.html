Introduction

This module covers the analysis of effective management in organizations with emphasis on the functions of management and the behavioral processes of change, conflict, leadership, motivation, communication, innovation, and group dynamics. Diversity, ethics, social responsibility, and international management are discussed. The module should allow students to develop a clear sense of how management and staff development contributes to the achievement of effective organizational outcomes.

Learning Outcomes

On completion of the module, students will be able to:

1. Demonstrate understanding of management's relationship to the organization and the individuals within it.

2. Evaluate the socio-psychological factors most influential in successful managerial leadership.

3. Identify the ethical and diversity aspects of management.

4. Evaluate the role of the manager in relation to the implementation and management of change.

5. Perform a self-evaluation of potential strengths and development areas in their individual approach to management, and prepare a management development plan.

Indicative Content

  • Organizational culture, functions and structure
  • Management and Leadership competencies Effective team management
  • Individual motivation and performance management
  • Communicating as a manager
  • Managing social responsibility and ethics, including diversity
  • Change and agents of change in organizations.

Assessment

Examination - 60%: LOs 1,2,3,4,5

Assignment - 40%: LOs 1,2,3,4,5
