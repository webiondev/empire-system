Introduction

This module is offered to new students in the BSc (Hons) Business Administration, whichever semester they begin their course of study.

The general aim of this module is to introduce students to the conduct of business research, which will be useful both when conducting academic business research for assignments and when conducting business research within and for organizations. In addition, students will be introduced to study skills at this level of study, in particular the use of literature. These skills and this use of literature relate to all modules in final year.

Students will study different ways of gathering, recording and analyzing business-related data and try to identify how the way data are collected and what kind of data are collected will be affected by the researcher's personal standpoint, prevailing paradigms in the business context and desired outcomes quantitative research methods (collection of data and analysis), interpretation for data and preparing the research report.

The mathematical content will include data presentation and computation of descriptive measures. If necessary, the statistical testing material from 2301 will be reviewed. This material include probability theory, probability distributions, expectations, variance, covariance, and correlation coefficient. If not covered in 2301, the following material will be included in this module: sampling, central limit theorem, statistical estimation, on or two sample tests of hypotheses. New topics introduced can be taken from: linear, time series, decision theory, and non-parametric methods. Data analysis may be performed using statistical computer packages.

The module will also progressively develop an understanding of:

The nature of research inquiry, encouraging analysis of the progressive and reflexive nature of inquiry from theoretical construction of the objects and questions of research to the strategic design of methods and tools of investigation.

A second stream of qualitative research methods, developing an understanding of a range of subject-specific research strategies including discourse analysis ethnography, action research and experimental methods.

Integration of scientific and judgmental forecasting in business applications.

Learning Outcomes

On completion of this module successful students will be able to:

1. Design business research studies for a variety of research questions, recognizing limits to objectivity, and set them in the context of published literature.

2. Gather primary and secondary study data, showing awareness of ethical, sampling and practical issues.

3. Analyse the data using appropriate quantitative and qualitative techniques.

4. Use the results of the analysis to make justified recommendations for business decision.

5. Present and report the results of the study using charts, statistics, tables as appropriate

6. Use historical and study data to perform basic business forecasting.

Indicative Content

The topics covered include:

  • Debates in research methods.
  • Qualitative versus quantitative methods.
  • Positivism and action research.
  • Ideas generation, reviewing and evaluation of literature.
  • Research approaches and strategies.
  • Access and ethical issues.
  • Sampling techniques.
  • Secondary techniques.
  • Secondary data sources.
  • Observational and interview research.
  • Questionnaire design and development.
  • Interview design and development.
  • Analyzing quantitative and qualitative data.
  • Evaluation of findings and methods.

Assessment

Assignment - 35%: LO1

Examination - 55%: LOs 2-6

Discussion Forum Participation - 10%
