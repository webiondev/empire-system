Introduction

The aim of the module is to enable students to develop the key skills necessary for use in the financial management of organizations and builds upon the learning outcome of module DB106 Fundamentals of Financial Control from the International Diploma in Business and module DBA 207 Applications of Financial Control from the International Advanced Diploma in Business.

This module will emphasis the acquisition and application of skills and knowledge for planning, decision making and control within an organization and provide the underpinning skills and knowledge for post-graduate and professional studies.

Learning Outcomes

On the completion of the module the student will be able to:

1. Identify, describe, evaluate and apply a range of appropriate management and financial control techniques

2. Diagnose and apply effective planning and decision making techniques and models to management problems involving organizational resources

3. Evaluate and distinguish between alternate courses of action using a range of models and techniques

4. Evaluate budgets, standard cost variances, reconciliation statements and performance indicators and measures

Indicative Content

  • The development, use and interpretation of financial rations for stakeholder analysis
  • The constitution, purpose and nature of working capital and the management of the working capital cycle
  • Capital investment appraisal methods and their role in the investment decition making process
  • Techniques and relevance of marginal, absorption and activity based costing, budgeting and budgetary control
  • The theory of divisionalisation and the application of transfer pricing
  • Performance indicators and performance measurement and management and the control and development of organizations
  • The financial implications of globalization and multi national commerce and trade

Assessment

Assignment - 40% Learning Outcomes 1,3

The assignment wil consist of the writing of a report on a financial and managerial problem facing the organization

Examination - 60% learning outcomes 2,4
