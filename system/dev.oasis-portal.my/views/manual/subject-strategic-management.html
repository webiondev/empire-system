Introduction and module retionale

The aim of this module is to develop student knowledge and understanding about hte role strategic management plays within organizations, its effects upon organizational performance and survival.

The module will focus upon strategic analysis by utilizing a set of analytical techniques for understanding better, and so influencing, a company's position in its actual and potential marketplace. The approach adopted encourages students to question and evaluate different perspectives of strategic management.

Learning Outcomes

On completion of the unit, students will be able to:

1. Explain what is meant by strategy.

2. Comment on the role that organizational vision, mission statements and strategic objectives play within strategic management.

3. Evaluate the relationship between the general and competitive environment.

4. Undertake an analysis of the general environment using PEST and scenario planning.

5. Undertake an analysis of the competitive environment using tools such as Porter's Five Forces framework and Strategic Group Analysis (SGA).

6. Evaluate the resource-based approach to strategic management.

Indicative Content

  • Strategic analysis and evaluation
  • Strategic planning process from strategy formulation to implementation.
  • Evaluation and analysis of the internal and external factors determining business strategy.
  • Application of a variety of strategic planning tools that aid in organizational strategic planning.
  • Strategic options available to management in the strategic selection process.
  • Strategic control and change management.
  • Organizational functions and responsibilities of departments in addressing and assessing the factors which affect the success of the organization.
  • Strategic formulation process and its applicability in different industry settings.
  • Managerial decision-making and communication skills.

Assessment

Assignment - 40%: LOs 2,3,4,7.

The assessment will consist of major project and presentation. The case analysis will assess the students ability to apply concepts learned in this module.

Examination - 60%: LOs 1,5,6.

In the final examination students choose 3 out of 5 questions which will assess their understanding of the subject matter.
