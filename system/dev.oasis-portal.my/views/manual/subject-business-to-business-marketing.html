Introduction

The aim of this module is to examine marketing between business organizations. The module will focus on the scope and nature of business marketing, unique aspects of business marketing strategy development and analysis of issues that face business marketeers.

Students will also study the core concepts for developing and maintaining internal and external customer relations, relationship marketing, customer satisfaction, quality, services marketing, consumer and industrial buyer behavior, personal selling, and ethical marketing conduct.

Learning Outcomes

On completion of the module, students will be able to:

1. Distinguish between the challenges of Business to Business (B2B) marketing as opposed to Business to Consumer (B2C) marketing.

2. Evaluate case studies and demonstrate an overall grasp of the marketing needs.

3. Discuss the importance of customer relationship management.

4. Outline different channels of customer communication and assess how they might be exploited for B2B marketing purposes.

5. Discuss the integral role of quality assurance in customer relationship management.

6. Demonstrate understanding of the psychological aspects of the industrial client.

7. Outline and discuss the ethical implications of B2B marketing.

Indicative Content

  • Marketing activity directed at organizational customers (business, non-profits, and government).
  • Organizational buying decision processes.
  • Marketing strategies, tactics and analytical tools relevant to marketing to organization customers.
  • Effective management of a sales force.
  • Building long term buyer/seller relationships.
  • Vertical strategies alliances.
  • Organization-level relationships.

Assessment

Assignment - 40%: LO 2

Examination - 60%: LOs 1,3-7
