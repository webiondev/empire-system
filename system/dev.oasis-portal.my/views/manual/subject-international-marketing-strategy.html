Introduction and module retionale

This module compares and contrasts export, international and multinational marketing. At the end of the course, students will be able to differenetiate between the marketing policies appropriate to industrialized, developing and underdeveloped economies. This module also aims to identify the sources of information, methods of information collection and the methods of information collection and the methods of information analysis suitable for international marketing planning.

Learning Outcomes

The student should be able to:

1. Compare and contrast export, international and multinational marketing.

2. Differentiate between the marketing policies appropriate to industrialized, developing and underdeveloped economies and to national and multinational marketing planning.

3. Identify and evaluate sources of information, methods of information collection and methods of information analysis suitable for international marketing operations.

4. Analyse the factors which distinguish product, price and promotional policy formulation for overseas markets as opposed to the domestic market.

5. Formulate methods of monitoring and controlling overseas selling and distribution.

6. Critically evaluate the impact of co-operative and restrictive measures to regulate international trade.

Indicative Content

The different models of international marketing:

  • The 4Ps - international differences.
  • The difference between national and international marketing plans.
  • International market research.
  • Marketing used in exporting.

Assessment

Assignment 40%: LOs 4,5,6.

Examination 60%: LOs 1,2,3.
